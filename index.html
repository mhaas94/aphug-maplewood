Topic 6.6 Part 1 — Density & Land Use

The Maplewood Redevelopment Authority has cleared three adjacent sub-districts near downtown for new construction. Your task: assign the correct housing density type to each sub-district based on its location, infrastructure access, and land cost. Drag each density type from the palette onto its matching sub-district.

Density types: High-density housing (apartments, high-rises) requires the most infrastructure per block but houses the most people — it is economically viable only where land costs are highest and transit access is strong. Medium-density housing (townhomes, small apartment buildings) balances land cost and population. Low-density housing (single-family homes, large lots) requires the most land per resident and is only financially viable where land is cheap and car access is assumed.
